About Burning Man

Burning Man is an annual event that takes place in the Black Rock Desert of Nevada. It began in 1986 when a small group of friends gathered on Baker Beach in San Francisco to burn a wooden figure as a ritualistic act of self-expression and community. Over the years, the event grew in size and popularity, ultimately evolving into the transformative experience it is today.

Our Mission

At Burning Man, our mission is to create a temporary community based on radical self-expression, art, and radical self-reliance. We aim to foster creativity, freedom, and connection, where participants are encouraged to express their true selves and engage in a vibrant exchange of ideas, art, and experiences. We strive to create a unique environment that challenges societal norms and encourages personal growth and exploration.

Our Principles

Burning Man is guided by a set of ten principles that define our community and its values:

  • Radical Inclusion
  • Gifting
  • Decommodification
  • Radical Self-reliance
  • Radical Self-expression
  • Communal Effort
  • Civic Responsibility
  • Leaving No Trace
  • Participation
  • Immediacy
