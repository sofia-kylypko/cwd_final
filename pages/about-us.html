About Burning Man

Burning Man is an annual event that takes place in the Black Rock Desert of Nevada. It began in 1986 when a small group of friends gathered on Baker Beach in San Francisco to burn a wooden figure as a ritualistic act of self-expression and community. Over the years, the event grew in size and popularity, ultimately evolving into the transformative experience it is today.

Our Mission

At Burning Man, our mission is to create a temporary community based on radical self-expression, art, and radical self-reliance. We aim to foster creativity, freedom, and connection, where participants are encouraged to express their true selves and engage in a vibrant exchange of ideas, art, and experiences. We strive to create a unique environment that challenges societal norms and encourages personal growth and exploration.

Community and Participation

Burning Man thrives on active participation, where attendees are encouraged to contribute, create, and collaborate. The festival fosters a strong sense of community, transcending traditional social structures and embracing radical self-expression. Participants are urged to engage with one another, offering gifts, talents, and experiences that enrich the collective gathering

The Temple and Burning of The Man

At the heart of Burning Man is the Temple, a sacred space for contemplation, remembrance, and healing. Each year, a new Temple is erected, providing a place for participants to reflect on personal losses, celebrate life, and connect with their emotions. On the final night of the festival, the iconic Burning of The Man takes place, symbolizing the release of inhibitions, the embrace of impermanence, and the collective energy that permeates the event.

Global Impact and Regional Burns:

Burning Man's influence extends far beyond the Black Rock Desert. Its principles and ethos have inspired a global community of Burners who organize regional events, known as "Regional Burns," around the world. These gatherings embody the spirit of Burning Man and create transformative experiences that promote self-expression, creativity, and communal living.
